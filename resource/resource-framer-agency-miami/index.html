What Is Framer?

Framer is a modern website builder designed for designers and developers who want full creative control without sacrificing performance. Unlike legacy platforms that layer plugin after plugin on top of a bloated CMS, Framer is built from the ground up to produce clean, fast, production-grade code.

It sits in a different category from tools like Webflow, Squarespace, or WordPress. The output is leaner, the animations are smoother, and the performance scores are consistently higher. For businesses that care about Google rankings, user experience, and first impressions, those differences matter.

Framer is used by design-forward companies across the world. Startups, SaaS companies, agencies, and service businesses are all moving to it because the gap in quality between a Framer site and a WordPress template site is immediately visible.

Here's why Framer consistently delivers that better than the alternatives:

Framer websites load fast because the platform generates clean, minimal code. This translates directly to higher Core Web Vitals scores, which Google uses as a ranking factor. A Framer site built by an experienced agency will routinely score 90+ on Google Lighthouse. Most WordPress sites, unless heavily optimized, fall well below that.

For Miami businesses competing for local search visibility, a faster website isn't just a better user experience. It's an SEO advantage that compounds over time.

Design Freedom Without the Developer Tax

Framer gives designers the ability to create pixel-perfect layouts, custom animations, and complex interactions without writing frontend code from scratch. The result is a website that looks custom-built, because it essentially is, at a fraction of the cost of a fully hand-coded build.

For Miami businesses, this is meaningful because it allows for visual quality that matches the city's aesthetic standards without the budget of a 500-person agency.

Easy Content Management

One of the common misconceptions about Framer is that it's hard to update. It's not. Framer has a built-in CMS that lets business owners update text, images, blog posts, and team pages without touching any code. For clients who need to publish content regularly, whether that's a resource blog, case studies, or team updates, Framer's CMS handles it cleanly.

Built for SEO from Day One

Framer handles the technical SEO fundamentals correctly out of the box: proper heading structure, canonical tags, fast load times, sitemap generation, and clean URL structures. When combined with intentional on-page SEO during the build, Framer sites rank well and rank faster than comparable WordPress builds.

What to Look For in a Framer Agency in Miami

Not every agency that lists Framer on their services page actually knows the platform. There's a meaningful difference between someone who has used Framer's drag-and-drop editor a few times and a team that understands how to build custom components, write override code, handle CMS architecture, and optimize the output for real-world performance.

Here's how to evaluate a Framer agency before you commit:

1. Ask to See Their Framer-Specific Portfolio

A legitimate Framer agency will have a portfolio of Framer builds. Ask specifically which projects were built in Framer and why. If they can't distinguish their Framer work from their WordPress work, or if everything in their portfolio looks the same, that's a signal they're using Framer as a template tool rather than a design system.

At Ocean Development, our Framer portfolio includes builds across multiple industries: biotech (AscendBioLabs), property platforms (MLS Global), fintech (Helix), and lending (PAR3 Lending Group, Investor Lending Solutions). Each one is built from scratch with a specific brand direction, not adapted from a Framer marketplace template.

2. Ask About Custom Code and Overrides

Framer has a feature called code overrides that allows developers to add custom React-based logic to any element on the page. This is where the real power of Framer opens up: custom animations, dynamic data fetching, form logic, interactive components. An agency that only uses Framer's visual editor is capped at what the standard toolkit can do. An agency that writes custom code can build almost anything.

If a Framer agency can't speak to code overrides, they're not a Framer development agency. They're a Framer template shop.

3. Ask About Performance and Lighthouse Scores

Any Framer agency worth hiring should be able to tell you the average Lighthouse scores on their recent builds. Ask for specific numbers. A well-built Framer site should be hitting 90+ on Performance, 95+ on Best Practices, and solid marks on Accessibility and SEO.

If they can't answer this question, their builds aren't optimized for performance and your SEO will reflect that.

4. Understand What's Included After Launch

A common gap in the Miami web design market is post-launch support. Framer pushes platform updates regularly, and occasionally those updates affect live sites. An agency that disappears after launch leaves you without anyone to catch those issues. Make sure post-launch support is clearly defined before you sign anything.

Framer vs. WordPress vs. Webflow: Which Is Right for You?

We get this question constantly. Here's the honest breakdown:

Framer is the right choice when you want a fast, visually distinct marketing site or landing page. It's ideal for businesses that want to look premium, rank well, and update content without a developer. It's our default recommendation for most Miami service businesses, startups, and agencies.

WordPress is the right choice when you need an extensive plugin ecosystem, a large content library, or a platform your internal team is already familiar with. It can be slow if not properly managed, but with the right technical setup it works well for content-heavy sites and businesses with existing WordPress infrastructure.

Shopify is the right choice for product-based e-commerce. It's not a direct competitor to Framer because it solves a different problem, but it's worth mentioning because many of our Miami clients come in asking about Framer for their e-commerce store. If you're selling physical products at volume, Shopify is the right platform. Framer handles marketing pages and brand sites, not inventory management.

Webflow is a capable platform that sits between Framer and WordPress in terms of complexity. We've used it, but we've largely moved away from it because Framer's design experience is cleaner, the performance output is better, and the platform has improved its CMS significantly over the past year.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hiring a Framer Agency

What is a Framer agency?
A Framer agency is a web design and development studio that specializes in building websites on the Framer platform. Not all web agencies use Framer, and not all agencies that list Framer as a service have deep expertise in it. A true Framer agency uses the platform as its primary build environment and understands its capabilities at a code level, not just a visual editor level.

How is Framer different from other website builders?
Framer produces cleaner, faster code than most drag-and-drop builders. It's used by design-forward companies because it allows for fully custom visual experiences without the performance overhead of platforms like WordPress or Webflow. The output is closer to a hand-coded site than most no-code tools.

Can I update my Framer website myself?
Yes. Framer has a built-in CMS that lets you update content, publish blog posts, and manage pages without touching any code. We configure this during the build process and walk every client through how to use it before launch.

Does Framer work for e-commerce?
Framer is best suited for marketing sites, landing pages, and content-focused websites. For full e-commerce functionality with product management, inventory, and payment processing, Shopify is the better choice. That said, Framer handles product showcase pages and brand marketing sites for e-commerce businesses very well.

Why should I hire a Framer agency instead of using a Framer template?
Framer templates get you 80% of the way there visually but leave the other 20% untouched: brand-specific design, SEO architecture, conversion optimization, custom interactions, and performance tuning. A Framer agency builds from your brand up, not from a template down. The difference shows in rankings, in conversions, and in how the site represents your business.
